Compliance and security
Gap analysis and audits: Consultants perform a comprehensive assessment of a client's current security posture to identify gaps between existing practices and CJIS requirements. They also help prepare agencies for triennial CJIS audits.
Policy development: This involves creating and reviewing policies to ensure they align with the strict CJIS security standards. These can include system security plans (SSPs) and documentation for all relevant security controls.
Risk assessment: Consulting companies help clients conduct regular risk assessments to identify, evaluate, and mitigate cybersecurity risks in their environments.
Incident response planning: They help develop and implement a framework for how clients should respond to and contain security breaches and other incidents involving Criminal Justice Information (CJI). 
Technology and implementation
System design and engineering: Consultants assist in designing and implementing robust, CJIS-compliant IT infrastructures, whether on-premises, in the cloud, or in a hybrid environment. This includes securing networks, implementing firewalls, and managing access control.
Data integration: This service helps agencies connect disparate data silos from various branches of the criminal justice system—including police, courts, and corrections—to provide a more complete and useful dataset.
System selection and procurement: Acting as vendor-neutral advisors, consultants help agencies assess, select, and procure new technology solutions such as Records Management Systems (RMS), Jail Management Systems (JMS), and Computer-Aided Dispatch (CAD).
Managed IT services: For agencies with limited internal IT staff, a CJIS consulting company can offer ongoing managed services, including maintenance, remote support, and cloud-based backups. 
Training and operational improvement
Security awareness training: Consultants offer tailored training sessions to ensure that all employees who handle CJIS data understand and can implement required security standards.
Operational analysis: This involves comparing a client's operational procedures with industry best practices and benchmarks to identify areas for improvement and increased efficiency.
Process improvement: Companies help optimize existing workflows for critical criminal justice functions, such as investigations, evidence handling, and records management, to ensure they are both efficient and compliant.
Specialized training: Some consultants with law enforcement backgrounds provide specialized training on topics such as use-of-force protocols, crime scene investigation, and tactical procedures. 
Strategic services
Law enforcement accreditation: Consultants can guide agencies through the complex process of achieving and maintaining law enforcement accreditation.
Strategic planning: This service helps criminal justice organizations develop long-term strategies to address complex issues and meet agency goals.
